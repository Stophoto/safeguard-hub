SG-SOP-002  Standard Operating Procedure

Washroom Escort Protocol

Doors open. Windows clear. Another adult knows where you are.
Document Code: SG-SOP-002 Version: 2.0 Issued: March 2026
Protect children and workers during bathroom breaks. Visibility and accountability apply at all times. When in doubt, call the parent.

Group Trips
Group Trips
  • Two screened adults escort every group.
  • Adults remain outside stalls, within sight and hearing of children.
Individual Trips
Individual Trips
  • Preferred: parent/guardian takes the child before or after class.
  • If a volunteer escorts a single child: one screened adult accompanies with the bathroom door propped open.
  • Another adult or hall monitor must remain in visual range.
  • Public restrooms: the escort waits in the common sink area (visible to others) and never enters a stall with a child.
Leaving the Room
When a Volunteer or Child Leaves the Room
  • Coverage must never drop below two approved adults (16+) in any classroom. Coverage is maintained by a floating staff member and floating volunteers, or by combining classrooms in an emergency.
  • When a volunteer needs to step out, or a child needs to use the washroom, get the attention of the floating volunteer in the hall first so they can cover or escort.
  • If there is no floating volunteer in the hall, use the walkie-talkie in the classroom to page staff.
  • Wait for coverage to arrive before leaving. Do not leave the remaining adult alone with the group.
  • If it is an emergency and you cannot reach anyone, have the children join the closest classroom so they are never left unsupervised.
Boundaries
No Private Situations
  • Never be alone with a child behind a closed door.
  • Never enter a locked cubicle with a child.
Diapering & Assistance
Diapering & Physical Assistance
  • Only in designated change areas with visibility (window or open door).
  • Always keep another adult nearby and aware.
  • Male volunteers are not permitted to change diapers.
  • Provide verbal assistance first. Physical assistance requires two adults present.
Contacting Parents
When in Doubt, Contact the Parent
  • When in doubt, the parent is contacted. Volunteers do not have a direct line to parents, so use the steps below.
  • Flag down the floating volunteer in the hall, or use the walkie-talkie to page staff, and ask them to contact the parent.
  • Diapering: if a diaper needs changing, have the floating volunteer or staff page the parent. If the parent does not arrive within a reasonable time, follow the diapering protocol above.
  • Toileting help or an accident (clothing change): let the floating volunteer or staff know so they can contact the parent as quickly as possible. If the parent cannot be reached in a timely manner, two adults assist the child together, following the same visibility rules as diapering.
  • Document any parent contact and the reason on the relevant session/check sheet.
Exceptions
Exceptions
  • If a privacy or safety exception occurs (e.g., medical emergency), record it in SG-OPS-001 (Admin Guide, Exception Log) and notify the ministry leader immediately.
The test: Doors open. Windows clear. Another adult knows where you are. If any of those three fail, stop and fix it.
