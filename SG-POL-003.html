Safeguard Framework

Bathroom & Diapering Protocols

Dignity · Visibility · Accountability
SG-POL-003 • Version 2.0 • March 2026
Purpose

Why This Policy Exists

Bethany Chapel safeguards children’s dignity and privacy while maintaining clear accountability for ministry personnel. These protocols establish how bathroom use and diapering must be conducted under the Safeguard Framework.

Policy

Core Protection Standards

  • Visibility: Maintain full visibility and accountability at all times.
  • No Isolation: An adult must never be alone with a child in a bathroom.
  • Doors: Keep bathroom doors open unless there is a clear, unobstructed viewing window.
  • Stalls: Never enter a stall or close a cubicle door behind you.
  • Hygiene: Wash hands before and after any diapering or toileting support.
Procedures

By Age Group

1. Nursery — Diapering (0–2 years)

  • Authorization: Only designated, screened female adults may change diapers. Male volunteers are not permitted unless caring for their own child.
  • Parent Preference: Diapering is standard service. If a parent has opted out, contact them when a change is needed.
  • Location: Changing must occur at approved diaper-changing stations only.
  • Visibility: Nursery door remains open. A second approved adult must be present or within line-of-sight.
  • Youth Helpers: Volunteers under 18 are not permitted to change diapers or assist with toileting.

2. Preschool — Bathroom Use (2–5 years)

  • In-Room Bathrooms: Door must remain propped open. Staff stand near the doorway but not inside unless assistance is required.
  • Hallway / Public Bathrooms: Use a single-stall family washroom first (leader waits outside). If a multi-stall restroom must be used: leader accompanies child to find a stall, then waits in the common sink area. Never enter a closed stall.
  • Physical Assistance: If help is required (wiping, zippers), two approved adults must be present. Attempt verbal guidance first.

3. Elementary — Bathroom Use (Grades 1–6)

  • Independent Use: Elementary children use the bathroom independently.
  • Notification: Child informs a leader before leaving. Leader notes the time.
  • Monitoring: If a child does not return within a reasonable time, a leader checks on them.

4. Special Situations

  • Vulnerable / Special Needs: Personal care assistance may only be provided when a second approved adult is present.
  • Off-Site Events: Same visibility principles apply. Two approved adults must be present whenever possible.
