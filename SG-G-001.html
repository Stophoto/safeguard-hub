Purpose

Why This Document Exists

The Safeguard Framework is the unified safety system for children, youth, and vulnerable adults across all ministries of Bethany Chapel. It establishes how ministries recruit, screen, train, and supervise workers who serve these groups.

This Governance Policy defines who leads the system, how it is maintained, and how training and renewal requirements are enforced—ensuring alignment with biblical values, best practices, and legal requirements.

Theological Foundation

Why We Protect

We affirm that every person is created in the image of God and deserves to be treated with dignity, respect, and care. Jesus welcomed children into his presence and taught that those who harm children bring judgment upon themselves. As a church family, we are called to a culture of care—stewarding those entrusted to us with proactive vigilance.

Definitions

Key Terms

  • Child / Youth: Any individual under the age of 18.
  • Vulnerable Adult: An individual who, because of age, illness, disability, or cognitive impairment, requires assistance or is at increased risk of abuse or neglect.
  • Safeguard Team: The designated group responsible for daily administration, oversight, and record-keeping of the Safeguard Framework.
Governance

Who Is Responsible

  • Board of Elders: Holds ultimate accountability for safeguarding, including final approval of all policies.
  • Executive Pastor: Provides operational oversight and ensures the Framework is implemented consistently.
  • Safeguard Team: Manages daily operation, monitors compliance, maintains secure records, and completes the annual compliance audit.
  • Ministry Leaders: Ensure staff and volunteers are equipped, trained, and following all Safeguard standards. Only trained and current workers may be scheduled.
  • Staff and Volunteers: Know and follow all Safeguard policies and procedures. Complete required training before serving and renew annually.

Training & Renewal

Requirements for All Workers

Before Serving

  • Complete Safeguard Training: All new volunteers must complete training covering the Core Protection Policies before serving.
  • Sign the Covenant: Complete SG-FRM-004 (Worker’s Covenant & Acknowledgement).
  • Confirm Clearance: You must be fully approved through the screening process (SG-POL-001) before training is valid for deployment.
  • Restriction: Do not serve in any child or youth role until all training and forms are complete.

Annual Renewal

  • Refresher Training: Complete refresher training each ministry year.
  • Policy Review: Review the Core Protection Policies to ensure you understand any updates.
  • Acknowledgement: Sign SG-FRM-004 confirming review and understanding.
  • Police Check Renewal: Renew Vulnerable Sector Checks every 3 years. Bethany Chapel covers the cost.
  • Non-Compliance: Volunteers who miss renewal deadlines must pause serving until current.

Role-Specific Training

  • Nursery / Preschool: SG-POL-003 (Bathroom & Diapering Protocols).
  • Check-In / Release: SG-SOP-005 (Receiving & Releasing Checklist).
  • Drivers / Transport: SG-SOP-007 (Transportation Procedures) and SG-FRM-010 (Driver Application).
Oversight

How the System Stays Current

  • Annual Audit: The Safeguard Team reviews compliance and recommends updates to the Board of Elders.
  • Incident Reporting: All incidents must be reported per SG-POL-004.
  • Compliance Monitoring: Ministry Leaders verify ongoing compliance within their areas.
