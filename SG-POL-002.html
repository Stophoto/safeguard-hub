Supervision Standards

SG-POL-002 • Version 2.0 • March 2026
Purpose

Why This Policy Exists

Bethany Chapel safeguards children, youth, and vulnerable adults by ensuring no one is ever left unsupervised or isolated with a ministry worker. This policy establishes the minimum adult presence required, the target supervision ratios, and procedures for handling coverage failures.

Section 1

The Two-Adult Rule

At least two approved adults must always be present when supervising children, youth, or vulnerable adults. In every room, on every trip, at every event, without exception.

  • Visibility: Activities must be observable and interruptible. Keep doors open and windows uncovered.
  • No Isolation: One-on-one situations between an adult and a child are prohibited.
  • Youth Helpers: Do not count toward the two-adult minimum.
  • Off-Site & Transportation: The Two-Adult Rule applies everywhere (see SG-SOP-007).
  • Bathroom & Diapering: Follow SG-POL-003 for detailed protocols.

Section 3

Coverage Drop Procedures

  • Temporary drop (e.g., bathroom break): Call for support before leaving. If unavailable, move the group to a visible, public space. Record on the Exception/Variance Log.
  • Structural failure (attendance exceeds capacity): Combine with another group or halt new check-ins. Do not operate an understaffed room.
Procedures

Before, During, and After

Before Program

  • Confirm two approved adults are present.
  • Consult SG-SOP-003 for your maximum participant count.
  • Count participants. Verify your team meets the target ratio.
  • Log attendance using SG-FRM-008.

During Program

  • Keep all spaces observable: doors open, windows uncovered.
  • If coverage drops, follow the procedures above.

If an Incident Occurs

  • Follow SG-POL-004 (Reporting & Response).
  • Complete SG-FRM-006 (Incident Report) the same day.
  • For suspected abuse, complete SG-FRM-007 and report to authorities immediately.
