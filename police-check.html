SG-FRM-003 · Police Check

Get your Police Check done.

You need a Police Information Check (with Vulnerable Sector Search) before you can serve with children, youth, or vulnerable adults. It's done online, it's valid for 3 years, and it usually takes about 15 minutes once your two documents are ready.
Two documents, two destinations — keep these straight and the rest is easy.
To police
Cover letter — you upload it to the police. It gets you the lower volunteer fee, so don't apply without it.
To church
Consent form — you sign it and give it back to the church (not the police). It lets the church receive your results.
Here's what to do

Follow these 5 steps to get cleared

Before you apply — get your two documents
  1. Download your cover letter
    Open your cover letter →
    It's already filled in with your details. Print it or save it as a PDF — you'll upload it to the police to get the reduced volunteer fee.
  2. Sign your consent form and return it
    Open your consent form →
    Sign it and hand it back to your coordinator. This is the church's copy — don't upload it to the police.
Apply online
  1. Go to the Calgary Police portal
    Visit policeinformationcheck.calgarypolice.ca (type it in exactly if it isn't clickable) and choose "Police Information Check with Vulnerable Sector Search — Volunteer."
  2. Have these ready
    Two pieces of government-issued ID (at least one with a photo)
    Your cover letter, to upload
    Enter Bethany Chapel as the organization
    Pay the reduced volunteer fee
After you apply
  1. That's it — results come straight to the church
    You don't need to chase anything down. Come back here and record the date you applied so your dashboard shows you're waiting on results.
Already have a recent check? If you've had a Police Information Check with Vulnerable Sector Search in the last 3 years, your coordinator may be able to accept it. Just ask before applying again.
