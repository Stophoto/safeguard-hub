SG-SOP-001  Standard Operating Procedure

Screening & Onboarding Checklist

Every step, every time — from application to approval
Document Code: SG-SOP-001 Version: 2.0 Issued: March 2026

No one serves in ministry with children, youth, or vulnerable adults until every step below is complete. This document describes the full screening and onboarding workflow — what happens at each stage, what it produces, and where the record lives in the admin panel.

Live Tracking
This is the reference. Actual tracking lives in the admin panel.

Use this document to understand the SOP. To record a volunteer’s interview, approval decision, or ministry assignment, go to the live tracker:

  • Admin · Screening roster — every volunteer’s stage at a glance; filter by interview-done, approved, assigned, etc.
  • Admin · People → click a volunteer → record interview / approval / ministry assignment inline on their profile.
  • Admin · Submissions — every SG-FRM-001 application lands here for coordinator review.

Coordinator-only. State is stored on each volunteer’s profile as profile.screening — a single source of truth across the Hub.

Screening Workflow

Seven steps, in order.

Each step produces a record in the admin panel shown at the bottom of its card.

1
Application
SG-FRM-001
  • Ministry Application (SG-FRM-001) received and reviewed.
  • Confirm at least 6 months of regular attendance (or elder-approved exception documented).
Tracked in:Admin · Submissions — every SG-FRM-001 submission lands here for review.
2
Interview
  • Schedule an in-person screening conversation (10–15 minutes).
  • Use the Interview Guide below.
  • Record interviewer name and date in the admin panel.
  • An interview is required before approval. In rare cases a coordinator may approve without one, but the system requires a written reason to be logged.
Recorded in:Admin · Screening → Interview section on the volunteer’s profile.
3
References
SG-FRM-002
  • Reference Check Form (SG-FRM-002) — collect and contact two references.
  • Document responses. Flag any concerns to Safeguard Coordinator.
Tracked in:Admin · People → Compliance → References on the volunteer’s profile.
4
Police Information Check
SG-FRM-003
  • Police Check Consent (SG-FRM-003) signed by applicant.
  • Volunteers download their own pre-filled cover letter from the police-check page — no manual issuing needed. (Confirm the letterhead details and signatory are set.)
  • Results received and filed. (Volunteer pays the reduced fee; the church can help if cost is a barrier.)
Tracked in:Admin · People → Compliance → Police Information Check.
5
Covenant & Acknowledgement
SG-FRM-004
  • Worker’s Covenant (SG-FRM-004) read, signed, and filed.
Tracked in:Admin · People → Compliance → Worker’s Covenant.
6
Training
SG-G-001
  • Volunteer completes Safeguard Training (volunteer track, SG-T-001 through SG-T-004).
  • Training completion recorded in tracking system.
Tracked in:Admin · People → Compliance → Safeguard training.
7
Final Approval
  • All documents on file and complete.
  • Ministry leader recommends applicant for approval.
  • Safeguard Coordinator or elder signs off.
Recorded in:Admin · Screening → Approval decision + Ministry assignment on the volunteer’s profile.

Appendix
Screening Interview Guide
This interview is a friendly, 10–15 minute conversation — not an interrogation. The goal is to hear the applicant’s story, understand their motivation, and prayerfully assess readiness for a role of trust. Choose a few questions from each category below.
i. Spiritual Background & Motivation
  • Can you share a bit about your faith journey?
  • What led you to apply to serve with children or youth?
  • What do you hope to offer through this ministry? What do you hope to grow in?
ii. Character & Readiness
  • What strengths do you bring to working with kids or youth?
  • What situations tend to challenge or frustrate you?
  • What does being trustworthy with kids and families mean to you?
iii. Judgment & Team Interactions
  • How do you respond when someone in leadership gives you feedback or correction?
  • Have you ever had to handle a situation where someone wasn’t following expectations? How did you respond?
  • Are you comfortable working as part of a team in flexible or fast-paced settings?
iv. Personal Integrity & Boundaries
  • Are there any past experiences, struggles, or personal challenges we should be aware of that could affect your ability to serve?
  • Is there anything you’d want leadership to know so we can support you well?
v. Closing & Next Steps
  • You’ll receive training on safety expectations and procedures. Is there anything that makes you feel nervous or unsure about volunteering?
  • Do you have any questions for us about the ministry or next steps?
