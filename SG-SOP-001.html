Screening & Onboarding Checklist

Every step, every time — from application to approval
Document Code: SG-SOP-001 Version: 2.0 Issued: March 2026

No one serves in ministry with children, youth, or vulnerable adults until every step below is complete. This checklist walks a ministry leader or Safeguard Coordinator through the full screening and onboarding workflow — from first conversation to final approval.

Workflow
Screening Workflow
Step 1 — Application
  • Ministry Application (SG-FRM-001) received and reviewed.
  • Confirm at least 6 months of regular attendance (or elder-approved exception documented).
Step 2 — Interview
  • Schedule an in-person screening conversation (10–15 minutes).
  • Use the Interview Guide on page 3 of this document.
  • Record interviewer name and date below.
Step 3 — References
  • Reference Check Form (SG-FRM-002) — collect and contact two references.
  • Document responses. Flag any concerns to Safeguard Coordinator.
Step 4 — Police Information Check
  • Police Check Consent (SG-FRM-003) signed by applicant.
  • Issue cover letter from SG-FRM-003 requesting Vulnerable Sector Check.
  • Results received and filed. (Church covers cost for approved volunteers.)
Step 5 — Covenant & Acknowledgement
  • Worker’s Covenant (SG-FRM-004) read, signed, and filed.
Step 6 — Training
  • Volunteer completes Safeguard Training (volunteer track, SG-T-001 through SG-T-005).
  • Training completion recorded in tracking system.
Step 7 — Final Approval
  • All documents on file and complete.
  • Ministry leader recommends applicant for approval.
  • Safeguard Coordinator or elder signs off.

Final Sign-Off

Appendix
Screening Interview Guide

This interview is a friendly, 10–15 minute conversation — not an interrogation. The goal is to hear the applicant’s story, understand their motivation, and prayerfully assess readiness for a role of trust. Choose a few questions from each category below.

Spiritual Background & Motivation
  • Can you share a bit about your faith journey?
  • What led you to apply to serve with children or youth?
  • What do you hope to offer through this ministry? What do you hope to grow in?
Character & Readiness
  • What strengths do you bring to working with kids or youth?
  • What situations tend to challenge or frustrate you?
  • What does being trustworthy with kids and families mean to you?
Judgment & Team Interactions
  • How do you respond when someone in leadership gives you feedback or correction?
  • Have you ever had to handle a situation where someone wasn’t following expectations? How did you respond?
  • Are you comfortable working as part of a team in flexible or fast-paced settings?
Personal Integrity & Boundaries
  • Are there any past experiences, struggles, or personal challenges we should be aware of that could affect your ability to serve?
  • Is there anything you’d want leadership to know so we can support you well?
Closing & Next Steps
  • You’ll receive training on safety expectations and procedures. Is there anything that makes you feel nervous or unsure about volunteering?
  • Do you have any questions for us about the ministry or next steps?
