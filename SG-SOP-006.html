SG-SOP-006  Standard Operating Procedure

Emergency & Injury Protocols

Read now. In a crisis, there isn’t time to look it up.
Document Code: SG-SOP-006 Version: 2.0 Issued: March 2026
Know your role before an emergency happens. Read this card now. In a crisis, you will not have time to look it up.
Evacuation
Evacuation (Fire, Gas, Unsafe Building)
Trigger: Fire alarm, gas smell, unsafe building condition, or elder/staff directive.
  • Direct children to line up at the door.
  • Take attendance clipboard or class list.
  • Escort group to the primary exit (posted on room map).
  • Reassemble at the designated muster point.
  • Take attendance again — confirm every child is accounted for.
  • Report any missing persons immediately to elder or emergency personnel.
  • Wait for emergency personnel or leadership instructions before re-entering.
Lockdown
Lockdown / Hold-and-Secure
Trigger: Intruder, external threat, police activity near site, or elder/staff directive.
  • Secure children inside the room.
  • Lock doors and turn off lights.
  • Silence all devices. Keep children quiet and away from windows.
  • Await instruction via phone or hallway messenger.
  • Do not open the door until you receive an official “all-clear.”
  • Debrief children calmly after the situation is resolved.
Injury Response
Injury Response
Minor Injuries (scrapes, bruises, small cuts, nosebleeds)
  • Provide basic first aid.
  • Notify the ministry leader.
  • Complete SG-FRM-006 (Incident Report).
  • Inform parents upon pickup.
  • Submit completed report before you leave for the day.
All bleeding injuries must be reported — even minor.
Moderate / Serious Injuries
Significant bleeding, head injury, suspected fracture, unconsciousness, or any injury requiring medical attention.
  • Do not move the child unless there is immediate danger.
  • Call 911 if needed.
  • Notify elder or senior staff immediately.
  • Complete SG-FRM-006 (Incident Report) and submit within 24 hours.
After any emergency or serious injury: the Safeguard Coordinator reviews the incident, documents lessons learned, and updates procedures if needed.
