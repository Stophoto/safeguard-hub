Volunteer Screening & Approval

What’s required before you serve

Bethany Chapel screens everyone who serves with children, youth, and vulnerable adults — not to put up hurdles, but because the people in our care deserve to be safe, and because you deserve to be supported and protected while you serve.

Here’s what that involves, in plain terms.

Your steps
What you’ll complete

Five things, once, before you start serving:

  • Ministry Application — a short form about you and where you’d like to help.
  • Two references — people who know you and can speak to your character.
  • Police Information Check — a standard background check (more on this below).
  • Worker’s Covenant — a one-time commitment to our standards of conduct.
  • Safeguard Training — short modules and a brief check, so you’re ready for real situations, not just informed about them.

Your dashboard tracks all of these and always shows you what’s next — so you never have to wonder where you stand.

Police check
About the Police Information Check

You’ll complete this after your interview and references. It’s valid for three years across any ministry role.

You cover the cost of your own check. If that’s a barrier, the church can help — just ask.

One honest line: anyone convicted of, or currently under investigation for, violent crimes or crimes against children or youth cannot serve in these ministries. No exceptions. It’s the clearest boundary we hold, and it’s there to protect the people you’d be caring for.
Timing
When can I start?

We normally ask volunteers to be part of Bethany for at least six months before serving with children, youth, or vulnerable adults. It simply gives us time to know one another.

If you’re a mature Christian transferring from another church, that wait can be waived — with three solid references, one of them from your previous pastor or ministry director.

While you’re finishing your steps, you may be able to help out under supervision.

Under 16
If you’re under 16

You’re welcome and encouraged to serve. You’ll complete full screening when you turn 16. Until then, you’ll always serve alongside approved adults.

Help
Questions?

Not sure about a step, or where you stand? Use the Have a question? button on any page — or tap below — and we’ll help you out.
