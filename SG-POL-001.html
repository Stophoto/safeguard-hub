Safeguard Framework · Policy

Volunteer Screening & Approval

Ministry Readiness · Background Checks · Onboarding
Document Code: SG-POL-001 Version: 2.0 Issued: March 2026
Purpose
Why This Policy Exists

Bethany Chapel is committed to equipping individuals called to serve in children’s, youth, and vulnerable adult ministries. Our screening process ensures every team member is prepared, supported, and authorized to serve.

Policy
Screening Requirements
Ministry Readiness

All ministry partners must complete the following before beginning service:

  1. SG-FRM-001 — Ministry Application (includes Youth Section)
  2. SG-FRM-002 — Reference Check (minimum two references)
  3. SG-FRM-003 — Police Check Consent
  4. SG-FRM-004 — Worker’s Covenant & Acknowledgement
  5. Safeguard Framework Training (see SG-G-001)
Police Information Checks
  • Completed following the initial interview and references.
  • Valid for 3 years for all ministry roles.
  • Bethany Chapel covers the cost.
Automatic Disqualification: Individuals convicted of, or currently under investigation for, violent crimes or crimes against children/youth will not be permitted to serve in any ministry involving vulnerable participants.
Youth Helpers

Youth helpers (under 18) are encouraged to serve. They do not count toward the Two-Adult Rule or supervision ratios until age 18, when they complete full adult screening.

Procedures
Step-by-Step
  1. Initial Interview: A ministry leader conducts an in-person interview using SG-SOP-001.
  2. Application: Applicant completes SG-FRM-001.
  3. References: Two or more references are checked using SG-FRM-002.
  4. Police Check: Applicant signs consent (SG-FRM-003) and submits for a Police Information Check.
  5. Covenant: Applicant signs SG-FRM-004.
  6. Training: Applicant completes Safeguard Framework orientation.
  7. File Review: The Safeguard Team reviews for final approval.
Exceptions
Early Service

Normally, volunteers must attend Bethany for at least six (6) months before serving with children, youth, or vulnerable adults.

The Safeguard Team may waive this wait for mature Christians transferring from another church, provided three (3) satisfactory references are submitted, one from their previous Pastor or Ministry Director.

Early-service volunteers may assist under supervision but do not count toward the Two-Adult Rule, may not lead alone, and must be marked as “early-service (in process)” on schedules.
