This is the final module. It covers the day-to-day operational rules you need to know: check-in and check-out, physical boundaries, digital boundaries, transportation, and emergencies. It closes with what you are committing to by signing the Worker’s Covenant.
Section 1

Check-In, Check-Out & Custody

Scenario

It is 11:05. A man you have never seen walks up and says, “I’m here to pick up Emma.” Emma’s mom usually picks her up.

What do you do?

Receiving children: Greet the family. Parent signs in on the attendance sheet (SG-FRM-008). Confirm the child’s name and check for any allergies or medical notes on the registration form (SG-FRM-005). If your room is at capacity, follow the Room Full protocol—do not accept “just one more.”

Releasing children: Verify the adult is on the authorized pickup list. If you do not recognize them, ask for identification—this is your job, not rude. Parent signs out. If someone is not on the list, keep the child with you and contact your ministry leader. The child does not leave until authorization is confirmed.

Late pickup: Stay with the child. Ensure two approved adults remain. Attempt to contact the parent. If unreachable, contact your ministry leader.

Custody situations: Follow the authorized pickup list exactly. If a parent says custody has changed, ask them to update the registration form with the ministry office. You cannot accept verbal changes. If a dispute arises at your door, keep the child with you, stay calm, and contact your ministry leader. You are not a mediator.

Section 2

Physical Boundaries & Discipline

Physical contact in children’s ministry is not prohibited—it is guided. All contact must be age-appropriate, brief, and in public view.

Appropriate

Side hugs (shoulder to shoulder). High-fives, fist bumps, handshakes. A brief pat on the upper back or shoulder. Holding a young child’s hand while walking.

Not Appropriate

Full frontal hugs. Kissing. Tickling, wrestling, or roughhousing. Allowing elementary-age or older children to sit on your lap. Any touch that is secretive, prolonged, or unwelcome.

The test: Would you be comfortable if a parent saw this interaction right now? If the answer is anything other than an immediate yes, change what you are doing.

Discipline: Redirect behaviour calmly. Use natural consequences—if a child throws a toy, the toy is put away. Never use physical force, yelling, shaming, or isolation. Never withhold food, water, or bathroom access. If a child’s behaviour is beyond your ability to manage, contact your ministry leader. Asking for help is not failure—it is the system working.

Section 3

Digital Boundaries

Everything you learned about visibility and the Two-Adult Rule applies to digital spaces. A private text thread is the digital equivalent of a closed room.

Do not text, direct message, or communicate privately with any minor you serve in ministry. This includes text, Instagram, Snapchat, WhatsApp, TikTok, Discord, or any other platform. If you need to communicate logistics, do so through a group message that includes a parent or ministry leader.

Social media: Do not follow, friend, or connect with minors on personal social media. If a minor sends you a request, decline it and let your ministry leader know.

Photos: Never photograph or record a child without written parental consent. Never post ministry photos on personal social media—those go on official church channels only.

Section 4

Transportation & Off-Site Events

Scenario

Youth group ends at 9 p.m. One teenager’s parents haven’t arrived. You offer to drive him home. You are the only adult left.

Every instinct says be helpful. The Safeguard Framework says: not alone.

The rule: Two approved adults in every vehicle carrying minors. An adult must never be alone in a vehicle with an unrelated minor without documented authorization. If a teen’s parents are late, wait with them in a visible space and contact the parents. Call another approved adult if needed.

Approved drivers must be on file through the Driver Application (SG-FRM-010) before transporting anyone for ministry purposes.

Overnight events: Same-gender adult supervision is required for sleeping arrangements. Adults must never share sleeping quarters with unrelated minors. No exceptions.

Section 5

Health & Emergencies

Before your first shift, know where the first aid kit, fire extinguisher, emergency exits, and muster point are in your ministry area. If you do not know, ask your ministry leader.

Sick children: If a child appears unwell, check with your ministry leader. Do not diagnose—just flag it.

Allergies and medication: Check the registration form (SG-FRM-005) before every shift for allergies and medical notes. Never administer medication without written parent consent on file. The only exception is emergency medication (EpiPen, inhaler) documented on the registration form.

Fire alarm or lockdown: Follow the posted emergency procedures for your room. In an evacuation: line up children, grab your attendance list, escort to the muster point, account for every child, do not re-enter the building.

Section 6

Your Commitment

You have now completed the Safeguard Framework volunteer training. Before you begin serving, you will sign the Worker’s Covenant (SG-FRM-004)—a personal commitment to uphold these standards for as long as you serve. Your completion will be recorded on the Training Completion Record (SG-FRM-012).

Thank you for taking this seriously.

By completing this training, you have added preparation to your motivation. You are not just willing to protect children—you are equipped to. What you do matters. Every Sunday. Every shift.

Welcome to the team.
