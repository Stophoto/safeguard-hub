Safeguard Framework

Reporting & Response

Mandatory Reporting · Documentation · Immediate Action
SG-POL-004 • Version 2.0 • March 2026
Purpose

Why This Policy Exists

Bethany Chapel ensures that all concerns of abuse, neglect, or injury are responded to immediately, legally, and compassionately. This policy reinforces our legal duty to report, our responsibility to protect, and our commitment to transparency.

Policy

Reporting Standards

1. Mandatory Reporting

Under Alberta law, any adult who has reasonable grounds to suspect a child is in need of intervention must report to Child & Family Services or the Police. This duty belongs to the person who witnesses or suspects—not to church leadership.

Reasonable grounds typically fall into three categories:

  • Disclosure: A child or vulnerable adult tells you they are being harmed.
  • Physical Signs: Unexplained bruises, burns, or injuries.
  • Behavioral Signs: Sexualized behavior, extreme fear of a specific person, or sudden drastic changes in behavior.

No Internal Filtering: Reports of abuse must not be filtered, delayed, or handled solely by church leadership.

2. Appropriate Touch

  • Appropriate: Side hugs, high-fives, fist bumps, handshakes, pats on upper back.
  • Inappropriate: Full frontal hugs, kissing, tickling, touching private areas, or allowing children to sit on laps.

3. Youth Helper Reporting

Volunteers under 18 must immediately tell their Ministry Leader or the Safeguard Team. The adult leader will assist in making the formal report.

4. Immediate Action

  • Safety First: If anyone is in immediate danger, call 911.
  • Do Not Investigate: Your role is to report, not investigate.
  • Timeline: Reports must be made immediately—do not wait.

5. Documentation

  • Refer to SG-SOP-004 (Reporting Flowchart) to determine the path.
  • SG-FRM-007 — Suspected Abuse Report: For any suspicion or disclosure. Record the child’s exact words.
  • SG-FRM-006 — Incident / Accident Report: For injuries, behavioral incidents, or property damage where abuse is not suspected.
  • Complete the appropriate form the same day.
Reporter Responsibilities

What You Must Do

  1. Listen calmly. Do not promise secrecy. Say: “I have to tell someone who can help keep you safe.”
  2. Support. Reassure them that the abuse is not their fault.
  3. Protect. If in immediate danger, call 911.
  4. Report to Child & Family Services or the Police immediately.
  5. Document. Complete SG-FRM-007 immediately.
Non-Retaliation

Protection for Reporters

Anyone who reports a concern in good faith will be fully supported and protected from retaliation, harassment, or discipline, regardless of the outcome of the investigation.

Quick Reference

Five Steps

  1. Ensure Safety (call 911 if immediate danger).
  2. Listen & Write Down (do not investigate).
  3. Report to Authorities (CFS or Police).
  4. Notify Safeguard Team (for support and accountability).
  5. Complete Documentation (SG-FRM-007 or SG-FRM-006).
